I believe these convention of basing time around the rotations of the ship is very reasonable. It make practical sense but I would imagine that the ship would not be always rotate at the same speed for the entire duration of the ships journey. Considering this is a fantasy world, it is entirely possible to have the ship maintain the exact same rotation speed for this journey but I will consider this not being the case. What I think may take this timing convention a step further is to base time around some nuclear mechanism. May it be nuclear decay or some other nuclear interaction. I believe ths may will leave room for less error and maybe be useful in scientific cases. I would try to find how long it takes for a certain element to decay into another within a minute, then use Earth's time convention to get a 24 hour equivalent, week and year equivalent.
